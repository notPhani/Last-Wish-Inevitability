# ⚡ Last Wish: Inevitability

*A game where power is a trap… and the only way to win is to let go.*

---

## 🧠 The Premise

The universe is already lost.

Goku has fallen.  
Black has won.  

With his final breath, Goku entrusts a single Dragon Ball to a boy —  
a boy with a familiar energy, but an unknown face.

You.

You are the last anomaly in a collapsing timeline.

---

## 🎮 What Kind of Game Is This?

**Last Wish: Inevitability** is a fast-paced 2D action game built around a single idea:

> What if getting stronger only brings you closer to failure?

- Dash-based combat  
- High-speed movement  
- Choice-driven progression  
- Multiple timelines  
- A final wish that changes everything  

---

## ⚔️ Core Gameplay

- Move freely across a fractured world  
- Dash toward enemies to attack  
- Chain actions to build momentum  
- Push your limits… and lose control  

The more you fight,  
the more unstable you become.

---

## 🧩 Choices That Shape You

Throughout your journey, you will face critical decisions:

- Train under Vegeta or walk alone  
- Save others or sacrifice them  
- Seek control… or embrace power  

Each choice alters:
- your abilities  
- your stability  
- your fate  

---

## 💀 The Twist

You cannot defeat Black.

No matter how strong you become.  
No matter what path you choose.

You will lose.

---

## 🧞 The Final Wish

After collecting all 7 Dragon Balls, you are given one chance:

> You can wish for anything.

Anything.

But every wish… still leads to your downfall.

Unless you realize the truth.

---

## 🔁 The Loop

After death, an angel offers you another chance.

You may rewrite your choices.  
Try again.  
Optimize better.

But the outcome does not change.

Until you understand:

> The system itself is the enemy.

---

## 🧠 What This Game Is Really About

- The illusion of control  
- The cost of power  
- The failure of optimization  
- Letting go to move forward  

---

## 🚀 Why This Game Exists

Most games reward you for getting stronger.

This one punishes you for it.

---

## 🎯 The Goal

Not to win.

But to understand *why you can’t.*
